Thesis Statement

- one sentence (or sometimes two) that clearly expresses the main idea or central argument of an essay

In [Sec. 2]: Not long ago, South Korea often made global headlines — and raised the ire of animal rights groups — for its tradition of breeding dogs for meat. But in recent years, people here have gravitated toward pets, especially dogs. They are looking for companionship at a time when more South Koreans are choosing to stay single, childless or both. More than two-fifths of all households in the nation now consist of only one person.


Explanation: This thesis statement explains that more South Koreans are keeping pets, especially dogs, because they seek companionship as fewer people are getting married or having children. It introduces the article's main argument and sets up the discussion that follows.
